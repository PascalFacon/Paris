Worth knowing
It’s the French national day. Morning military parade on the Champs-Élysées, fireworks at the Eiffel Tower in the evening, and the Bal des Pompiers (firemen’s ball) the night before and night of. Many museums are open, but the city is busy — expect crowds and metro disruptions near official events.

Practical Info
Good to know
Getting around: The hotel is 2 min from métro Brochant (line 13) and 6 min from La Fourche (lines 13 and 13B). A carnet of 10 t+ tickets is around €17 — better value than buying singles. The Navigo Easy card is a cheap reloadable alternative.
Neighbourhood — Batignolles: A villagey corner of the 17th. Daily market on Rue Lemercier, Parc Martin Luther King for greenery, lots of cafés and small restaurants. Quiet by Paris standards, still very walkable to central sights.
Money: Cards work almost everywhere, contactless included. Some small bakeries and markets still want cash — keep a few coins.
Weather in mid-July: Typically 20–28°C, occasional warmer days. Light clothes, comfortable shoes, a small bottle of water.
